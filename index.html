Highlighted Research

At Stanford, I am involved in research at the Stanford Smart Sensing (S4) Lab and the Navigation and Autonomous Vehicles (NAV) Lab. My work here has focused on utilizing Synthetic Aperture Radar for passive backscatter communication and 3D Gaussian Splatting for plant segmentation. You can find more details about my research here: Projects

Synthetic Aperture Radar for Passive Backscatter Communication
I implemented a processing pipeline that takes L0-RAW Sentinel-1 data and breaks it into several sublooks. The amplitude of a modulating corner relfector allows us to send bits through the received backscatter by the satellite. My work involved automating the sublook generation process, verifying amplitude stability and calculating the radar cross section from the raw data itself. This work allowed us to perform faster deployments and testing of our system.

Improving Plant Segmentation in 3D Scenes using Gaussian Splatting and Clustering
I developed optimization algorithms that benchmark optimizers that use Gaussian Splatting to better understand how the software interprets geometry and color in a scene. This allowed me to understand the root causes of issues such as glare, which are a crucial weakness in most 3d reconstructions. To improve scene segmentation, I implemented density based clustering algorithms (DBSCAN, HDBSCAN, OPTICS). The process was optimized by downsampling techniques in order to train the machine learning algorithms faster. The results of this work can be applied to any scene segmentation task.
